Dispute Resolution Policy

Last Updated: January 24, 2026 | Version 1.0

Fair, transparent process for resolving conflicts between landlords, tenants, and roommates

Introduction

RoomFinderAI provides a structured dispute resolution process to address conflicts between users. This policy outlines how disputes are filed, reviewed, mediated, and resolved.

Important: This policy applies to disputes between Users (landlord-tenant, roommate, sublease conflicts). For disputes with RoomFinderAI itself, see our Terms of Service - Section 10.

3-Step Resolution Process

1

Informal Resolution (7 Days)

Goal: Resolve the issue directly between parties before escalating

✓

Communicate through Platform messaging

Attempt to resolve directly with the other party (documented communication)

✓

Clearly state your concern

Explain the issue, what went wrong, and what you'd like to happen

✓

Allow 7 days for response

Give the other party reasonable time to address your concern

Success Rate: ~60% of disputes resolve during this phase when parties communicate in good faith.

2

Formal Dispute Filing (3-5 Business Days)

If informal resolution fails: File an official dispute through our platform

1

Submit dispute form

File your dispute with details, evidence, and desired outcome

2

Receive case number

Unique identifier (DSP-2026-XXXXX) for tracking your case

3

Initial review

RoomFinderAI team reviews within 3-5 business days

4

Notify respondent

Other party notified and given chance to respond with their evidence

Required Information:

  • Dispute type (scam, security deposit, harassment, etc.)
  • Detailed description of what happened
  • Evidence (screenshots, photos, documents, messages)
  • Desired resolution outcome
3

Mediation & Resolution (10-15 Business Days)

Platform mediator facilitates resolution: Neutral third party helps both sides reach agreement

M

Mediator assignment

Trained RoomFinderAI mediator assigned to your case

📊

Evidence review

Mediator reviews all submitted evidence from both parties

💬

Facilitated communication

Mediator helps parties communicate through dispute messaging system

✅

Resolution proposal

Mediator proposes fair resolution based on evidence and platform policies

Possible Outcomes:

  • Mutual Agreement: Both parties agree to resolution terms
  • Refund/Compensation: Financial remedy ordered
  • Warning Issued: Violating party receives warning
  • Account Suspension: Temporary or permanent ban for severe violations
  • Listing Removal: Fraudulent/violating listings taken down
  • No Action: Insufficient evidence or no policy violation

Your Rights During Disputes

✅ You Have the Right To:

  • Submit evidence and documentation
  • Respond to accusations with your side of the story
  • Upload additional evidence during mediation
  • Request updates on case status
  • Appeal decisions you believe are unfair
  • Withdraw your dispute at any time
  • Confidentiality of dispute details
  • Representation (you can have someone help you)

❌ You Do NOT Have the Right To:

  • Directly contact the mediator outside the platform
  • Demand specific punishments for the other party
  • Publicly share dispute details or evidence
  • File frivolous or harassing disputes
  • Retaliate against the other party
  • Guarantee of your preferred outcome

Evidence Guidelines

Strong Evidence Includes:

  • Screenshots: Messages, emails, text conversations (with timestamps)
  • Photos/Videos: Property condition, damages, repairs needed
  • Documents: Lease agreements, receipts, itemized statements, notices
  • Communication logs: Platform messages, call records
  • Financial records: Payment confirmations, bank statements, Venmo/Zelle transactions
  • Third-party reports: Inspection reports, police reports, code violation notices

Pro Tip: Document everything in real-time. Take photos when you move in, save all messages, and keep receipts. Evidence collected during the dispute is often less credible than contemporaneous documentation.

File Upload Limits:

  • Maximum 10 files per dispute
  • 5MB per file
  • Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, PDF, DOCX
  • Files are stored securely and only visible to dispute parties and mediator

Appeals Process

If you believe the resolution was unfair or based on incorrect information, you may appeal:

  1. Submit appeal within 14 days of resolution notification
  2. Provide new evidence or explain why the decision was incorrect
  3. Appeals reviewed by senior mediator (different from original mediator)
  4. Decision final after appeal (no further appeals)

Important: Appeals are rarely overturned unless significant new evidence emerges or procedural errors occurred. The appeals process is not a "second chance" to argue the same points.
