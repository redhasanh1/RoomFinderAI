Complete Student Housing Guide

Make informed decisions about your housing options with our comprehensive comparison tools and expert guidance.

Dorms & On-Campus

Traditional campus living experience

Pros:

  • • All-inclusive (utilities, internet, food)
  • • Close to classes and campus activities
  • • Built-in social opportunities
  • • 24/7 support and security
  • • No furniture or setup needed

Cons:

  • • Higher cost per square foot
  • • Limited privacy and space
  • • Strict rules and regulations
  • • Mandatory meal plans
  • • Limited storage space

Average Cost:

$8,000 - $15,000/year

Includes room, board, utilities

Off-Campus Apartments

Independent apartment living

Pros:

  • • More space and privacy
  • • Often cheaper than dorms
  • • Cook your own meals
  • • Choose your roommates
  • • Real-world living experience

Cons:

  • • Separate utility bills
  • • Commute to campus
  • • Furniture and setup costs
  • • Less social interaction
  • • Lease responsibilities

Average Cost:

$400 - $1,200/month

Plus utilities, groceries, furniture

Shared Houses

Group living with multiple roommates

Pros:

  • • Lowest cost per person
  • • More space than apartments
  • • Built-in friend group
  • • Shared household responsibilities
  • • Often includes yard/outdoor space

Cons:

  • • More potential for conflicts
  • • Shared bathrooms/common areas
  • • Group liability for damages
  • • Harder to find quality houses
  • • Coordination challenges

Average Cost:

$300 - $800/month

Per person, plus shared utilities

Student Renters Insurance Guide

Why You Need Renters Insurance

  • • Protects personal belongings from theft or damage
  • • Covers additional living expenses if displaced
  • • Provides liability protection for accidents
  • • Required by many landlords and lease agreements
  • • Often very affordable for students ($10-20/month)

What's Typically Covered

  • • Electronics (laptop, phone, gaming systems)
  • • Clothing and personal items
  • • Textbooks and school supplies
  • • Bike and sports equipment
  • • Temporary housing if your place is uninhabitable

How to Get Coverage

1. Check Parents' Policy

You might be covered under parents' homeowners insurance while in school

2. Get Student Discounts

Many insurers offer special rates for students

3. Compare Quotes

Shop around with multiple providers for best rates

4. Document Your Belongings

Take photos/video of valuable items for claims

Student Tenant Rights

Know your rights as a student tenant and learn how to protect yourself legally.

Student Tenant Rights Overview

Your Rights Include:

  • • Protection from discrimination based on student status
  • • Right to habitable living conditions
  • • Security deposit return within legal timeframe
  • • Privacy rights and advance notice for inspections
  • • Protection from retaliatory eviction

Common Violations:

  • • Refusing to rent to students
  • • Withholding security deposits without cause
  • • Entering without proper notice
  • • Failing to make necessary repairs
  • • Discriminatory lease terms for students
